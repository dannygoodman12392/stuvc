# Where Stu is, and what to build next
**2026-07-16.** Written after competitor research (Harmonic, Affinity, Attio) and a full measurement of production.

---

## 0. The one-paragraph version

Stu's supply problem is fixed: 585 facts now flow onto cards that were empty this morning. Its **output** is still zero. The two columns the product exists for — Stu's READ and Danny's CALL — are **0 of 183 each**. The conviction engine has been live 27 hours and has never scored a company. Danny's own judgment, the only asset here that no competitor can buy, is **structurally gated behind a $2 AI run**. Meanwhile the one thing that cannot be bought or backfilled — longitudinal history — is being **actively destroyed on every refetch**.

Nothing below is a big build. The expensive work is done. What remains is mostly ungating, recording, and not throwing things away.

## 1. What the research actually says

Three findings, each of which changes a decision.

### 1a. At pre-seed, the company has no data exhaust — only the founders do

Harmonic is the inspiration, so it's worth being precise about what a Harmonic profile of a 4-person company contains: **name, website, a scraped description, 2 LinkedIn profiles, and founder background badges.** That's it. Everything that makes the product feel like a product is empty or noise:

- `traction_metrics` — meaningless. Headcount deltas on a base of 4 people: one hire is +25%. `headcountEngineering.ago90d.percentChange` on 2 engineers is a rounding artifact, not a signal.
- `web_traffic` — below any provider's measurement floor.
- `funding_rounds` — empty (and a paid add-on; they resell Crunchbase and PitchBook for it).
- `related_companies` — poor, since similarity is computed from a description that barely exists.

Their own stealth-detection methodology confirms it. The signals they name — *departure from a high-signal employer*, *no public landing*, *cluster of ex-colleague hires*, *newly registered domain* — are **all people-graph signals, not company signals.** And the tell: they run `harmonic.ai/get-discovered` asking founders to self-submit. If passive detection worked at this stage, they wouldn't need to ask.

**Consequence:** any card design that leads with company traction renders mostly empty rectangles. Stu's card already leads with The Team — that instinct was right. Keep it.

### 1b. Neither Affinity nor Attio can read a meeting — architecturally

Affinity's own security page: email bodies are encrypted such that *"the plaintext never exists on Affinity database servers at any point in time."* Their graph is built on **envelope metadata and subject lines**. Attio has no transcript corpus at all.

This is not a gap they haven't gotten to. It is a constraint they engineered into themselves for multi-tenant privacy reasons, and it is permanent. Danny's ~336 transcripts/year are a corpus **neither vendor can legally or architecturally touch.**

And the researcher, with no knowledge of Danny's first-call script, independently named the resulting feature:

> *"Longitudinal claim diffing. Same founder, three meetings, six months apart — did the story change? No CRM models 'what they said last time vs. this time.' Their data model has no slot for it."*

Danny, months earlier, unprompted:

> *"The delta between what they said and what they did is my single best signal — it's the one thing they can't perform."*

Two independent parties arrived at the same conclusion. That is the moat, and Stu now has the substrate for it.

### 1c. Both CRMs converged on Stu's two-author rule — and both got two things wrong

Affinity and Attio independently landed on the same provenance rule Stu already has: **a human edit is sticky and terminal; it permanently disables machine sync for that cell.** Two products converging independently is decent evidence the rule is right. Stu's version — "Danny owns the columns, the machine owns the blob" — is the same idea.

Where they fail, and where Stu can be better cheaply:

1. **They replace instead of diffing.** Affinity's is actively lossy: overwrite an enriched cell and the machine's value is *gone*. The documented way to get it back is to **blank the cell and wait 24 hours**. The undo is a side-effect of emptiness. That is a bad interaction and Stu should not copy it.
2. **No staleness model.** An enriched value from 2024 renders identically to one written this morning. No "as of" on the cell.
3. **Confidence exists in exactly one place** in either product — Attio's Web research agent, which returns confidence + reasoning + citations on hover. That is the bar. It applies to ~4 attribute types and nothing else.
4. **Two authors is the wrong number.** See §3e.

**The free, abundant, proprietary thing is gated behind the expensive, rare, commodity thing.** That is why the compounding dataset has never started compounding, and it is the single most important thing in this document.

## 3. What to build, ranked

### A. Stop destroying history — append-only snapshots · **URGENT · small**

`company_enrichment` is a single blob, overwritten on every refetch:

```sql
UPDATE founders SET company_enrichment = ?, company_enriched_at = CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
```

The 44 companies enriched today — 276 people, headcounts, arrival curves — will be **destroyed** by the next run. Not archived. Overwritten.

Harmonic's research is unambiguous that this is their one un-buyable asset: *"You cannot backfill this. If you start today, you have zero history today and three years of history in three years."*

Every day without this is a day of history that can never be recovered. It is also what makes "how are they doing" computable at all — a derivative needs two readings. Danny asked for that on day one and it is currently unanswerable by construction.

The pattern already exists in-house: monitors do universe → snapshot → diff → alert, and `founder_memos` already has a `data_snapshot` column.

**Scope:** one append-only `company_snapshots` table (founder_id, taken_at, source, headcount, blob, content_hash); write on every enrich + public-record read; skip a write when the hash is unchanged. ~30 lines.

### B. Ungate Danny's call · **small**

Let Danny record a band + one-line rationale + a dated prediction **from the card, in five seconds, with or without an assessment.** Keep the server's existing refusal to accept a decision with no prediction — that refusal is the teaching.

Why this is #2 and arguably #1: calibration needs *his* call first. Stu's read is worthless without something to check it against, and his call is free, instant, and available on all 183 cards today. Every week this stays gated is a week of judgment that evaporates.

### C. Re-run the 9 carded assessments · **trivial · mechanical**

~$2 × 9, ~30 minutes. Makes the board's most important column real, and validates the engine on production for the first time.

### D. The Q10 delta — claim diffing across calls · **medium · the moat**

The substrate now exists: typed, quote-backed, dated claims per call. ONNYX already has two calls; tonight's backfill will produce more.

Diff a founder's claims across meetings and surface the drift on the card:
> *May: "six customers." July: "three pilots."*

Neither Affinity, Attio, nor Harmonic can build this — the first two can't read a meeting, and the third has no transcripts. Danny already named it as his single best signal. Nothing else in this document is defensible; this is.

**Prerequisite:** 2+ calls per founder. Tonight's run gets there for several.

### E. The third author · **medium**

Both CRMs model {machine, human}. Stu has a third: **transcript-derived** — machine-*extracted* from a human primary source. It deserves its own class because it's the only one that can cite **a moment**, not just a domain:

> *"$40k MRR — founder, 2026-03-12 @ 14:22"*

That is strictly better provenance than anything either vendor ships, and Stu gets it for free. It also gives real epistemic ranking: when Crunchbase says Series A and the founder said "we're still on a SAFE," that isn't a data-quality bug — it's two sources of different standing, and the transcript is **primary**. Both CRMs flatten that to one winner and a colour.

**Scope:** per-fact `{machine-fetched | user-entered | transcript-derived}` + source + as-of + confidence. Never destroy the machine value on override — show a dismissible diff, not a deletion. Do not copy Affinity's "blank the cell and wait 24 hours" revert.

### F. Kill the dead weight · **trivial**

132 of 183 cards are Stage 5. The board says 183; the real pipeline is **51**. And 19 Density/SSFI cards are still there against an explicit instruction to remove them.

Default the board to live; put Stage 5 behind a filter. Drop Density.

### G. Founder highlights · **small · high leverage**

The one block that genuinely lights up at pre-seed, per §1a. Stu now has **276 people with prior employers** — already paid for, already stored.

Harmonic's highlights (Prior Exit, Top University, Major Tech Company Experience, Deep Technical Background, Prior VC-Backed Founder, Seasoned Founder) are the **least defensible part of their product** — string-matching LinkedIn history against a curated list. A weekend gets 80%.

The value isn't the computation, it's **the curation**: which companies and schools count *for Danny's thesis*, which is his taste, encoded. That's the part worth building by hand.

### H. Aesthetic · **small**

- The card's right rail is a column of empty fields (Role, Email, Illinois tie, ARR, Burn, Runway, Valuation, Round size, We invested). **Empty fields teach you the tool is empty.** Hide unfilled machine fields behind "show all"; keep what Danny actually fills.
- Attio's editing model is the reference: typed attributes → derived editors, commit-on-blur, optimistic writes, keyboard-first. Their stated rationale for keyboard-first is **data quality** — friction is why CRMs rot. That's the right argument and it applies here.
- Steal Harmonic's `funding_attribute_null_status`: absence modelled as a **typed value**, not a null. Same instinct as Stu's evidence rung. It's the most sophisticated thing in their schema.

## 4. Open risk

**EnrichLayer / Proxycurl.** Proxycurl was reportedly sued by LinkedIn and shut down; EnrichLayer appears to be the successor, and Stu's entire enrichment path depends on it. Continuity and legal exposure are **unverified** as of this writing — research in flight. If it's fragile, §3A (snapshots) matters even more: history you've already captured survives a vendor disappearing.

---

## 5. What NOT to build

- **Company traction metrics.** Noise at 4 people. Harmonic's own numbers are meaningless at this stage.
- **A relationship graph.** Affinity's product taxonomy is *"People You Know"* vs *"People Your Allies Know."* For a solo, allies = 0 and the second half is permanently empty. The $2k/seat is priced against a network effect that requires colleagues. (Caveat: if Superior Studios colleagues' inboxes are ever in scope, this changes.)
- **Out-resolving Harmonic.** Their real moat is entity resolution + longitudinal maintenance of a 195M-person graph. That's an ops and cost problem, not an algorithm. Don't compete there. They'll never have the transcripts.
